The Backwards Law
The pursuit of positive experience is itself a negative experience, and the acceptance of negative experience is itself a positive experience. By accepting negative experiences rather than constantly chasing positive ones, we paradoxically achieve greater satisfaction.
Happiness Is a Problem
Happiness comes from solving problems, not avoiding them. Negative emotions serve a purpose as signals. The key is to find problems you enjoy solving rather than expecting a problem-free life.
You Are Always Choosing
Taking responsibility for everything in your life - even things outside your control - is liberating. When we feel we choose our problems, they become more manageable. This mindset shift transforms victimhood into empowerment.
Failure Is the Way Forward
The "Do Something Principle" teaches that action precedes motivation, not the other way around. Small actions create momentum. Failure is not just inevitable but essential for growth and success.
And Then You Die
Confronting mortality clarifies what truly matters. Death is the ultimate context that gives meaning to our choices. By regularly questioning our mortality, we can focus on what's truly important rather than trivial concerns.
